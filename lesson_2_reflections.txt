What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?
You start a .git file that then needs to be filled. So you can have copies
of your work.

How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository?
What value do you think it offers?
The working directory holds everything, all the files. The repository is only
going to hold copies of what you've chosen to track. The staging area is where
you can choose what to add and when.

How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical
change?
You are able to compare what is in the staging area, working directory, and
commits. So you can decide what/how much to save.
